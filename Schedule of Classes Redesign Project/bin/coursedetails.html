Credit Hours: 2 lecture hours; 4 lab. hours; 4 credits
Description: This course is a general survey of the diversity, adaptation, and evolution of life. Fundamental principles underlying the science of biology are studied to convey an appreciation of the evolutionary trends among the kingdoms and of the interaction of organisms with the environment. In the laboratory, students examine monerans, protists, fungi, plants, and animals, both living and preserved. (Not open to students who have taken BIO 2003.)

This course may not be taken with the Pass/Fail option.
Course Comments: Course has a lab and lecture.
Pre-requisite: BIO 3001 (2020) and departmental permission.
